### Self-Listening and Self-Talk

A part of me feels overwhelmed by the amount of tasks at hand.

A part of me feels that they are all necessary.

I hear both parts and understand both perspectives.

Let's investigate this together, let's see which tasks are necessary and in which way and try to sort them and maybe eliminate or reduce them if possible.

It's no use telling myself to just be less stressed, it is like telling a country with closed borders to become open to foreigners. They may not have the infrastructure to implement that: airports, large enough streets, places to accomodate visitors. The people might not know how to approach different cultures or talk to them or accept them without feeling that their culture is threathened by the ideas of the visitors. They might have to learn new languages which may be very hard for old citizens. They might have to accomodate with the fact that people they meet on the street do not share the same values as them and make an effort to adapt to that. The way people interact has to change, the way they think has to change, the infrastructure of the country has to change. It takes a lot of time and effort and experience and knowledge. Similary, your stress response evolved over millenia to ward off danger from predators. We're limited, we cannot have millions of neurons dedicated for each type of interaction. The same stress response we've developed to deal with lions in the jungle, now gets called whenever we (or something which is important to us: status, possesions, loved ones) feel endangered. You can tell your organism to be less stressed, but what does that mean? It means new neural pathways, new ways of mananing hormones, new ways for cells to interact with each other, new skill for then cells themselves to learn. But what neural pathways? What hormones and in which way? In which way should the interaction between the cells work so that you don't mess up your other systems which are regulated by the same hormones? In which way should the interaction between the cells change such that you're still able to deal with a dog wanting to bite you? The infrastructure is limited, there's a limited amount of neurons and limted resources in the body, what should be thrown out to make room for implementing new responses to certain situations? Just telling yourself to "be less stressed" means nothing. It answers none of these questions, it provides no guidance, resources nor abilities to implement any of these changes. It's good to set a purpose, but its very hard to define it clearly, and a lot harder to actually implement it. It takes time, effort, experience, information and physical resources. This is why, I think, you can just wish to be fit, you have to go do the sport to teach your body in what way to be fit, and you have to invest the time, effort and resources to accumulate experience and knowledge.
