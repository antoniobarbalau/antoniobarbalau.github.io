# Extended Naturalism


## Standard Naturalism

There is a performative contradiction in (i.e. one cannot perform the action of) living a meaningful life with your spouse while upholding a worldview in which everything is just atoms in the void. In Standard Naturalism, in the current standard scientific worldview, love is nothing but a chemical reaction, love is an illusion, love is not real. We are just a bunch of atoms bouncing off one another in a cold, meaningless container. So why would loving your partner have any meaning? Why would living have any meaning? What is the purpose of science in an Universe in which there are just atoms moving meaninglessly around in the void? It seems to me that the current scientific worldview does not propose an onthology in which the pursuit of science makes sense. So why pursue it?

The scientific revolution and the enlighenment brought us, alongside this materialistic onthology, the dichotomy, the clear separation between the subjective and the objective, while emphasizing that only the objective is worth pursuing, only objective is true, only the objective is real. The clear separation between fact and values, where only the facts are true, only the facts are real, only the facts are worth pursuing. In such a materialistic worldview, where there is such a great separation between us as subjects and the objective universe, where there is no continuity between the subjective and the objective, there is little chance of escaping solipsism (the only thing I can ever know is that I exist, "Cogito, ergo sum"), skepticism and nihilism.
