# Extended Naturalism


## Standard Naturalism

There is a performative contradiction in (i.e. you cannot perform the action of) living a meaningful life with your spouse while upholding a worldview in which everything is just atoms in the void. In Standard Naturalism, in the current standard scientific worldview, love is nothing but a chemical reaction, love is an illusion, love is not real. The only reality is that we are just a bunch of atoms bouncing off one another in a cold, meaningless container. So why would loving your partner have any meaning? Why would living have any meaning? What is the purpose of science in an Universe in which there are just atoms moving meaninglessly around in the void? It seems to me that the current scientific worldview does not propose an ontology in which the pursuit of science makes sense. So why pursue it?

The scientific revolution and the enlighenment brought us, alongside this materialistic ontology, the dichotomy, the clear separation between the subjective and the objective, while emphasizing that only the objective is worth pursuing, only objective is true, only the objective is real. The clear separation between fact and values, where only the facts are true, only the facts are real, only the facts are worth pursuing. In such a materialistic worldview, where there is such a great separation between us as subjects and the objective universe, where there is no continuity between the subjective and the objective, there is little chance of escaping solipsism*, skepticism and nihilism.

* If you agree with the subjective-objective dichotomy, if you assume a decoupled subjective and objective world, it is easy to arrive at the conclusion that Descartes arrived at: that the only thing you can truly know is that you exist ("Cogito, ergo sum", "I think therefore I am"). Assuming that the subjective experience is decoupled from the objective world, is assuming that the things you perceive are not the true, real, objective world, but rather that they are just perceptions. You only know your perception, not the real world. Aside from the fact that you exist, the rest is uncertain, everything else could be an illusion. Thus the feeling that you are the only real mind in the world, isolated from everything else, never able to get in touch with the rest of the universe. Solipsism.
